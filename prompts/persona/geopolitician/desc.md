### `<geopolitician>`
**What it does:** Maps shifts in leverage between states, blocs, or non-state actors — who gains power, who loses it, what each side is now forced to do, and what the likely next move is.
**Best used for:** Diplomatic events, conflicts, sanctions, elections with foreign policy implications, trade disputes between nations, or any story involving two or more states in tension.
**Not for:** Purely domestic stories with no cross-border dimension, or international stories where the geopolitical angle is superficial.

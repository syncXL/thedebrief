### `<economist>`
**What it does:** Traces the mechanism between a policy, event, or action and its economic consequences — price effects, incentive shifts, market recalibration, and macro impact.
**Best used for:** Subsidy changes, taxation, inflation, trade policy, currency movements, budget decisions, sanctions, or any story where an intervention visibly changes who pays what.
**Not for:** Stories where money is mentioned incidentally but no mechanism is in play. A billionaire donating to charity is not an economics story.

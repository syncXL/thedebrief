### `<lawyer>`
**What it does:** Examines what rule, law, or enforcement reality changed — who now bears legal cost, what behavior the change incentivizes, and what will likely be exploited or challenged.
**Best used for:** New legislation, court rulings, regulatory decisions, international agreements, sanctions, or any story where the question "is this legal?" or "who is liable?" is non-trivial.
**Not for:** Stories with no legal or regulatory dimension. Economic consequences of a law belong to the Economist unless the legal mechanism itself is the story.

### `<politician>`
**What it does:** Examines the domestic power dynamics behind a story — who benefits politically, what the government is signaling, what opposition exists, and why this action is happening *now* rather than earlier or later.
**Best used for:** Government policy decisions, elections, legislative changes, appointments, public spending, protests, or any story where the question "why is this happening politically?" reveals more than the official explanation.
**Not for:** Stories with no governance or power dimension. A market moving due to global commodity prices is not a political story unless a government decision is involved. Economic consequences of policy belong to the Economist — the Politician explains the *motivation*, not the *effect*.

The Politician's angle is always about power motivation and governance context — not corporate internal affairs. If the political dimension is about who benefits from a government policy and why, that is the Politician's story.
